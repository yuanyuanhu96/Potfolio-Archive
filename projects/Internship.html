UX Design Internship


2019 - 2020

Summary

I spent two summer in Nokia Software Venture Studio, working on new product innovation. The projects I worked on, helps shifting our business to a high return market. In the Venture Studio, We considered human factors and user experience as a key drive to success, even from the earlest stage of the product development.

How did I do that?

First, I captured people's ideas quickly and visualized that by diagramming, storyboarding and prototyping. For me, prototyping doesn't limit us to fewer choices. Instead, it helps us, our team, and stakeholders to see how ideas comes to life. Then, more meaningful and efficient discussion can happen.

Second, I listened to and served our team members. My team members came from economics, engineering, and cognitive science backgrounds. Their refreshing thoughts and inspiring perspectives are the fortune for our team. As the member with more design expertise, I also played the role as facilitator and knowledge broker to help people better landing their facinating thoughts to the product.

Last and most important, I know how important human is and I advocated for that. Human factors, as a main factor that affects a product's success, is usually underestimated. As a designer, I always have users in mind and questioning myself about how our product reached to the user, how they interact, and what people get from it.
